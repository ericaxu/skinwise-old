What It Is

Mandelic acid is one of the largest alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs), and derived from bitter almonds. In other areas of healthcare it is used as an antibacterial, but for skincare, it's useful for treating irregular pigmentation, acne, photoaging and mature dry skin.

Like other AHAs, mandelic acid can be used as a leave-on treatment at lower concentrations (typically 2-10%) or a peel at higher concentrations. However, mandelic acid stands out because it's quite mild, and is suitable for sensitive skin and darker skin prone to postinflammatory hyperpigmentation than the more common glycolic acid. It also works well in peels for acne in combination with salicylic acid.
